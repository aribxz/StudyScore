Under the Hood
How the model works
A peek at the feature engineering and maths behind the predictions.

The scoring formula

The model was trained on 200 synthetic student records using scikit-learn's LinearRegression. The key insight is that study hours don't have a fixed effect — they interact with sleep quality. Studying while sleep-deprived yields far lower returns than studying well-rested.

Score = study_effect # 2×, 4×, or 5× hours (by sleep) + 3 × practice_tests − 4 × phone_usage − 10 × sleep_bad + 10 × sleep_good + noise

Sleep is encoded as two binary flags: sleep_bad (<6 hrs) and sleep_good (6–8 hrs). These interact with study hours to create non-linear effects inside a linear model — feature engineering.

The 7 model features

📖
Hours Studied
Raw hours per day. Multiplied by 2, 4, or 5 depending on sleep quality.
📝
Practice Tests
Each test adds ~3 points. Strong linear relationship.
😴
Sleep Bad + Sleep Good (flags)
Two binary features encoding whether sleep is in the <6 or 6–8 hour range.
⚡
Hours × Sleep (interactions)
The key feature engineering step — captures how sleep modifies study effectiveness.
📱
Phone Usage
Each hour of phone usage removes ~4 points from the predicted score.
